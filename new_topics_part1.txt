Diverting the tram produces the good (the five saved) directly, with the one's death as a side flowing from it; pushing the man produces the good only <em>through</em> his being hit. The distinction is subtle to the point of vertigo, and Kamm defends every joint of it against a battery of invented cases.</p><p>Why bother with such filigree?
